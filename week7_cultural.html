Week 7 Stereotype Revisited



(Week 7) Revisiting Stereotype Threat

I think my ability to address stereotype threat relates back to having a growth mindset. As a reminder, the growth mindset perceives talents and abilities as things that can be developed over time. This is in contrast to a fixed mindset, which believes that abilities and intelligence are pre-determined traits that are simply fixed. I believe that maintaining a growth mindset will help to offset stereotype threat since stereotype threat is all about a belief that a group of people inherently have some characteristic that makes them intrinsically good or bad at some activity. In the case of Asian students, there is a positive stereotype dictating that they will outperform their peers on standardized testing. However, I believe that their success has more to do with the culture of hard work and productivity. Thus, I constantly remove myself from the belief that I am intrinsically bad at some thing because of my ethnicity, gender, etc., and instead believe that through enough deliberate practice, I can become good at whatever it is I'm seeking to accomplish.

In terms of what I will do to make sure that I maintain this growth mindset:
I will include exercise as part of my daily routine, in order to release stress. I will also respect the wisdom of my body and realize that there are times when giving myself rest or taking a break from a project will actually help me to be more productive.
